- **Before a customer meeting:** Review 01 and 02 to be fluent on what GHAS delivers on GHES today and why it's worth purchasing standalone.
- **During the meeting:** Use 04 (one-pager) as the primary conversation artifact.
- **For the "move to cloud" conversation:** Lead with 03 — cover GHEC-only features, then present the hybrid model as a low-risk path.
- **For the "why buy GHAS at all" conversation:** Lead with 02 — tool consolidation, developer experience, and compliance readiness.
- **Leave-behind:** Share 04 (one-pager) and 01 (feature comparison) with the customer.

## Key Takeaways

1. **GHAS on GHES is a smart purchase on its own** — replaces 3–5 tools, integrates security into developer workflow, and delivers value regardless of cloud plans.
2. **GHEC unlocks AI-powered remediation (Copilot Autofix)** — the single most differentiated capability.
3. **A hybrid model (GHES + GHEC) is a viable path** — customers can move incrementally, project by project, at their own pace.
4. **The feature gap widens over time** — GHES quarterly releases mean customers are always 3–6 months behind.
5. **GHAS skills and configs transfer directly** between GHES and GHEC — no re-work needed when migrating.
