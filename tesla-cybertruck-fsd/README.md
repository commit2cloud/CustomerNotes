# Tesla FSD v14 on the Cybertruck: In-Depth Overview for Prospective Buyers

> **Current FSD version as of late 2025:** FSD v14.1.7 (software update 2025.38.8.7)  
> **Hardware:** All Cybertrucks ship with HW4 (AI4) — Tesla's latest self-driving computer

---

## 🧠 What Is FSD?

Tesla's **Full Self-Driving (Supervised)** is an advanced driver-assistance system (ADAS) that uses an end-to-end neural network trained on millions of real-world driving clips. It handles steering, acceleration, and braking — but **requires full driver attention at all times**. It is **not fully autonomous**.

---

## ✅ What FSD CAN Do on the Cybertruck

### City & Highway Driving
- Navigate city streets and highways including lane changes, merges, and exits
- Handle roundabouts, unprotected left turns, U-turns, and complex intersections
- Dynamic rerouting around road closures or real-time hazards
- Emergency vehicle detection and response
- Debris avoidance

### Parking & Low-Speed Maneuvers
- **Autopark**: Parallel and perpendicular parking — both pull-in and head-in
- **Auto Unpark**: Back out of a parking space and pull away autonomously
- **3-Point Turns**: FSD can handle full 3-point turns with automatic Drive/Reverse shifts
- **Smart Summon**: Navigate parking lots and come to you via the Tesla app
- **Park in lot, street, driveway, garage, and curbside** (v14+)
- Self-initiated departure from parked position ("Start Self-Driving" from park)

### Awareness & Detection
- All onboard cameras running at 36 Hz for 360° situational awareness
- Vulnerable road user (pedestrian, cyclist) detection
- Vision-based lane detection (no radar/lidar — camera-only)
- Automated camera cleaning alerts for obstructed sensors

### Personalization
- **Speed/style profiles**: Chill, Average, Assertive, **Sloth** (extra cautious), **Mad Max** (aggressive)
- Adjustable following distance and lane change behavior
- Real-time driver profile adjustments mid-trip
- Enhanced touchscreen UI with better onboarding for new users

## ❌ What FSD CANNOT Do (Limitations)

| Limitation | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Full autonomy** | Always requires an attentive, licensed driver ready to take over |
| **Tight multi-point parking maneuvers** | Cybertruck's size (~232" long) makes tight spots challenging |
| **Construction zones** | Performance can be inconsistent; manual takeover often needed |
| **Unusual road markings** | Faded, temporary, or ambiguous lanes may confuse the system |
| **Adverse weather** | Heavy snow, fog, and heavy rain degrade camera performance |
| **All regulatory jurisdictions** | FSD availability varies by region and local law |
| **Guarantee of perfection** | Driver intervention is still sometimes necessary |

## 🛡️ Safety

### The Good News
- Tesla reports **up to 7x fewer collisions** with FSD Supervised engaged vs. manual driving
- **5x fewer off-highway incidents** with FSD active
- End-to-end AI training continuously improves from real-world edge cases
- Regular OTA updates — the system gets safer without visiting a dealer

### How Driver Monitoring Works
- **Hands-on-wheel detection**: The steering wheel monitors hand pressure — you must maintain grip
- **Cabin camera**: Eye-tracking monitors driver attention; repeated inattention leads to FSD suspension
- **Escalating warnings**: Visual alerts → audio alerts → FSD disengagement if you're not paying attention
- **Misuse consequences**: Tesla can permanently revoke FSD access for egregious misuse

### When to Take Over
- Construction zones or unusual detours
- Unclear road markings or missing lane lines
- Tight parking maneuvers beyond the truck's comfort zone
- Any time you feel unsure — the system is designed to hand control back instantly

## 🎯 Tips & Tricks for New Tesla / EV / Cybertruck Owners

### FSD & Autopilot Basics
- **Engage FSD**: Double-tap the right stalk (or tap "Start Self-Driving" on screen in v14)
- **Disengage**: Single tap the right stalk up, or brake/steer to override
- **Try all profiles**: Start with "Average," try "Chill" for a relaxed experience, and "Assertive" only when comfortable
- **Route editing**: You can tap waypoints on the map during FSD navigation to add stops
- **Turn signal for lane changes**: FSD makes the final decision, but a turn signal is your "request"

### Range & Energy Management
- **Use the Energy app**: Monitors real-time efficiency, projected arrival range, and where energy is going (climate, terrain, speed)
- **Precondition before charging**: Navigate to a Supercharger in the app/car; the truck will pre-heat the battery for maximum charge speed
- **Precondition before driving in cold**: Use scheduled departure or the app to warm the cabin and battery before you leave — saves miles
- **Reduce HVAC drain**: Use seat heaters instead of cabin heat where possible; they're far more efficient
- **Regenerative braking**: Default "one-pedal driving" harvests energy back into the battery — let off the accelerator smoothly

### Cybertruck-Specific Features
- **Off-Road Mode**: Adjusts suspension height, power split, and unlocks locking differentials for trail driving
- **Cybertent/Camping Mode**: Self-level the truck with suspension adjustments, then use Jack Mode to freeze it in place for a stable camp setup
- **Air compressor**: Built-in to inflate tires on the trail
- **Frunk access**: Can be opened via the app, touchscreen, or voice command ("Open frunk")
- **Tonneau cover**: Improves highway range efficiency; opens/closes from the touchscreen or button
- **Rear camera zoom**: Tap the rear camera feed to cycle zoom levels (1.0x → 1.25x → 5.0x) — great for towing visibility

### App & Voice Commands
- **Tesla app**: Lock/unlock, climate control, charging management, Summon, and FSD supervision from your phone
- **Voice commands** (press the right microphone button):
  - "Navigate home"
  - "Set temperature to 70"
  - "Open glovebox"
  - "Schedule charging"
  - "Call [contact name]"
- **Sentry Mode**: Records external cameras when parked — enable for security, disable in your garage to save battery

### General EV New-Owner Tips
- **Charge at home if you can**: A Level 2 charger (NEMA 14-50 or hardwired 48A) is the best home charging setup
- **You'll rarely visit a gas station**: Most owners charge overnight at home 90%+ of the time
- **Don't charge to 100% daily**: For daily driving, keep it at 80–90%; 100% is for long trips only — preserves battery longevity
- **Don't let it sit at 0%**: Keep it above 20% if parking for extended periods
- **OTA updates happen automatically**: Schedule updates for overnight to avoid disruption. Accept updates promptly — they often include FSD improvements
- **Frunk as grocery bag**: The front trunk is waterproof and great for storing cold items

> **Bottom line**: FSD v14 on the Cybertruck is genuinely impressive and gets better with every OTA update. Combined with the Cybertruck's unique capabilities (off-road, PowerShare, insane towing), it's one of the most capable vehicles on the road today.
